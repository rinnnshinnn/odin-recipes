Pork Sinigang Recipe

Description

Pork sinigang is a traditional Filipino soup known for its tangy and savory flavors. It features pork simmered with a variety of vegetables such as radish, eggplant, tomatoes, and okra, all cooked in a tamarind-based broth. The tamarind gives the soup its distinctive sour taste, which is balanced by the savory richness of the pork. Often served with rice, pork sinigang is a comforting and hearty dish enjoyed for its unique and refreshing flavor profile.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 (½ inch) piece fresh ginger, chopped
  • 2 plum tomatoes, cut into ½-inch dice
  • 1 pound bone-in pork chops
  • 4 cups water, more if needed
  • 1 (1.41 ounce) package tamarind soup base (such as Knorr®)
  • ½ pound fresh green beans, trimmed

Steps

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion; cook and stir until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes. Season with salt.
  2. Stir in ginger, tomatoes, and pork chops. Cover and reduce heat to medium-low. Turn the pork occasionally, until browned.
  3. Pour in water and tamarind soup base. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the pork is tender and cooked through, about 30 minutes.
  4. Stir in green beans and cook until tender.
